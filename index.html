Course Overview

The ICT in Health Informatics course provides an in-depth understanding of the pivotal role Information and Communication Technology (ICT) plays in transforming healthcare systems. It covers the application of technology in managing health information, facilitating clinical decision-making, improving patient outcomes, and ensuring seamless communication between healthcare providers.
Students explore foundational concepts such as electronic health records (EHRs), health information exchanges (HIEs), and telemedicine, alongside emerging technologies like mobile health (mHealth), artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, and big data analytics. The course emphasizes system interoperability, data privacy, and security, highlighting how these elements contribute to building efficient and patient-centered healthcare systems.
Through case studies, practical assignments, and discussions, students gain hands-on experience with ICT tools and their real-world applications. This prepares them to address challenges in healthcare delivery, such as optimizing workflows, reducing costs, and enhancing access to care, particularly in resource-constrained settings.
By the end of the course, students are equipped with the skills to lead technological innovations in health informatics and contribute to the development of smarter, more efficient healthcare solutions.
